US Energy Analysis · 2024–2030

Data Center Load Growth & Geographic Concentration

AI and data center expansion is the dominant driver of US electricity demand through 2030, accounting for roughly 55% of all incremental load growth. Nationally, data centers represent ~4% of total US electricity. This significantly understates localized impacts.

Load is concentrated in a handful of states and counties. In Northern Virginia, data centers already consume 27% of state electricity and are projected to reach 39–57% by 2030. Eight states are projected to exceed 20% DC share by 2030 (EPRI, Feb 2026). Infrastructure costs are being socialized across utility service territories far beyond the host counties, driving rate increases for residential customers.
